Christ the Redeemer


The Christ the Redeemer statue is an Art Deco masterpiece that overlooks Rio de Janeiro from the 710-metre peak of Mount Corcovado in the Tijuca Forest National Park. Completed in 1931 after nine years of construction, the monument stands 30 metres tall (excluding its 8-metre pedestal) with arms spanning 28 metres, made of reinforced concrete covered in a mosaic of thousands of triangular soapstone tiles for durability and texture. The statue was a collaborative effort between Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and French-Polish sculptor Paul Landowski, funded almost entirely by donations from the Brazilian Catholic community as a symbol of peace and Christianity. As one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, it features a hollow interior with a small chapel at its base and is equipped with numerous lightning rods to protect it from the frequent strikes it sustains during tropical storms.
